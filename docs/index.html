Hardware Hacking

deep

Hardware hacking and embedded security news in May 2026 highlights significant vulnerabilities and increasing sophistication in attacks. Advantech products, including SaaS Composer, IoTSuite Growth, IoTSuite Starter, IoT Edge, and WebAccess/SCADA, were found to have SQL injection vulnerabilities (CVE-2026-6888) that could allow remote authenticated attackers to execute arbitrary commands. Additionally, Intel and AMD released Patch Tuesday updates addressing 70 vulnerabilities across their product portfolios, with Intel patching a critical buffer overflow (CVE-2026-20794) in its Data Center Graphics Driver for VMware ESXi, which could lead to privilege escalation and code execution. AMD also addressed a critical flaw (CVE-2026-0481) in its Device Metrics Exporter, exposing port 50061 to unauthenticated users. Furthermore, a Linux kernel local-root flaw named Fragnesia (CVE-2026-46300) was disclosed, affecting all supported AlmaLinux releases through the IPsec ESP and rxrpc modules, allowing an unprivileged process to gain root access.

Supply chain security remains a critical concern, with the Nitrogen ransomware gang claiming to have stolen 11 million files, totaling 8TB of data, from Foxconn, a major Apple and Nvidia partner. This stolen data reportedly includes confidential instructions, project details, and schematics for products from Intel, Apple, Google, and Nvidia. The incident underscores the rising risk of physical infrastructure attacks and hardware backdoors within the supply chain. New hardware hacking tools like HaleHound, a DIY multi-protocol offensive security toolkit with WiFi, Bluetooth, SubGHz, 2.4GHz, and RFID/NFC support, are also emerging, making powerful hardware more accessible for security research and penetration testing. The Flipper Zero (2026 edition) and HackRF continue to be essential multi-protocol radio tools for IoT pentesting.
