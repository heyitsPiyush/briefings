Exploit development news as of May 2, 2026, highlights several critical vulnerabilities and exploit techniques. A significant local privilege escalation (LPE) flaw in the Linux kernel, dubbed "Copy Fail" (CVE-2026-31431), was publicly disclosed and actively exploited in April 2026. This vulnerability, present since Linux kernel 4.14 in 2017, allows an unprivileged local user to achieve root access by performing a controlled 4-byte write into the page cache of any readable file, primarily targeting setuid binaries through the AF_ALG interface and splice() system call. A public proof-of-concept (PoC) in Python, approximately 732 bytes, demonstrates reliable exploitation across major Linux distributions like Ubuntu, Amazon Linux, RHEL, and SUSE, and is particularly concerning for cloud and containerized environments due to shared kernel page cache.

Another notable vulnerability is CVE-2026-41940, a critical authentication bypass in cPanel & WHM, with a CVSS score of 9.8. This flaw, exploited in the wild since at least February 2026, allows unauthenticated remote attackers to gain administrative access by manipulating the whostmgrsession cookie through a Carriage Return Line Feed (CRLF) injection. Google Chrome also saw a major security overhaul, patching 30 vulnerabilities in late April 2026, including four critical flaws. Many of these were "Use-After-Free" (UAF) memory corruption issues, such as CVE-2026-6919 in DevTools, which could allow a compromised renderer process to escape the browser sandbox. Additionally, CISA has added several actively exploited flaws to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog, with federal agencies mandated to patch them by early May 2026, including vulnerabilities in Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Manager.
