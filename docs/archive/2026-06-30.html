deep

Recent hardware and embedded security news highlights critical firmware vulnerabilities and escalating IoT threats. A significant authentication bypass flaw, CVE-2026-35075, has been identified in firmware utilizing hard-coded passwords, allowing remote unauthenticated attackers to gain administrative access by extracting default credentials. Qualcomm's Cq8750m firmware is also affected by CVE-2026-25277, a buffer overflow in its Strongbox component that can lead to memory corruption, impacting Snapdragon mobile platforms, FastConnect Wi-Fi chips, and modem subsystems. Furthermore, Marlin Firmware versions up to 2.1.2.7, when configured with MESHBEDLEVELING, contain an out-of-bounds write vulnerability (CVE-2026-56111) in the M421 G-code handler, enabling attackers to corrupt firmware memory and cause denial of service or state corruption on devices like 3D printers through crafted G-code commands.

IoT security remains a major concern, with growing attack surfaces comprising billions of connected devices, many of which ship with default credentials and infrequent updates. Forescout's 2026 research indicates routers and switches average 32 vulnerabilities per device, accounting for 34% of critical vulnerabilities in enterprise networks. Supply chain security saw a major incident with Tata Electronics, an Apple supplier, suffering a hack where over 630 gigabytes of confidential data, including iPhone 18 Pro details, were stolen and leaked. RF hacking continues to be relevant, with new research demonstrating the ability to exploit vulnerabilities in wireless protocols, such as those found in AirDrop and Quick Share, enabling nearby attackers to crash services or bypass session checks on Apple and Android devices.
